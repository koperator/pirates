1. Game Loops
Micro (Combat): Spot marker → close distance → position broadside → select ammo → fire → board/sink.
Meso (Voyage): Leave port → consume supplies per leg → return to port before running out of hull/crew/supplies.
Meta (Progression): Sell loot → repair → recruit → upgrade → buy new ship.
2. Controls & Camera
Camera: Locked to player. Math: cam += (ship - cam) * 0.1
W / S: Forward thrust / Brake (reverse at reduced power).
A / D: Turn left / Turn right.
Left Click: Fire broadside.
Aim Logic: If mouse X/Y is left of the ship's forward vector, fire Port. If right, fire Starboard.
1 / 2 / 3: Select ammo type.
Space: Attempt boarding.
3. Ship Physics
Formulas (Fixed Timestep):
code
JavaScript
speed += thrust * dt;
speed -= linearDrag * dt;
speed = clamp(speed, -maxReverse, maxSpeed);

turnSpeed += turnInput * angularAccel * dt;
turnSpeed -= turnSpeed * angularDrag * dt;
turnSpeed = clamp(turnSpeed, -maxTurnSpeed, maxTurnSpeed);

speed -= Math.abs(turnSpeed) * turnSpeedBleed * dt; // Turning slows ship
heading += turnSpeed * dt;
Damage/Load Multipliers: Max Speed and Max Turn Speed scale down based on Sail HP%, Hull HP%, Crew shortage, and Cargo load.
4. Ship Classes
Sloop: Very Fast, High Turn, Low HP, 2-3 cannons/side, 6-10 crew.
Brigantine: Fast, Good Turn, Med HP, 4-6 cannons/side, 10-15 crew.
Brig: Medium Speed, Med Turn, Med+ HP, 6-8 cannons/side, 16-22 crew.
Frigate: Med-Slow, Slow Turn, High HP, 10-12 cannons/side, 28-32 crew.
Galleon: Slow, Very Slow Turn, Very High HP, 12-18 cannons/side, 30-40 crew.
5. Combat & Ammo
Volleys: Spawn from ship center in a cone (no individual cannon sockets). Consumes 1 Powder per click. Reloads per side.
Ammo Types:
Round Shot: Long range, tight spread. (High Hull DMG).
Chain Shot: Medium range, medium spread. (High Sail DMG).
Grape Shot: Short range, wide spread. (High Crew DMG).
Collision: Strict Circle-vs-Circle. Ship collision radius = ~65% of visual length.
6. Crew & Morale (Rum)
Stats: crewCount (Int) and crewQuality (Float 1.0 to 3.0).
Power Base: Efficiency = crewCount * crewQuality.
Manning Penalty: If crewCount < minCrew for the ship, apply severe reload/turn penalties.
Morale States:
Well Supplied: 100% efficiency.
Rationed: 85% efficiency.
Dry: 65% efficiency.
Dry + No Food: 50% efficiency, boarding disabled.
7. Resources & The "Leg" System
Resources: Gold, Food, Rum, Powder, Cargo (Int max capacity).
Drain Mechanic (No per-second drain): Food and Rum are consumed instantly every time the Encounter Director spawns a new marker (a "Leg").
Cost per Leg: Food = crewCount, Rum = ceil(crewCount / 3).
8. Encounter Director & World
World: Infinite X/Y Canvas space. No procedural map.
Director: Hidden timer (10–22s). On tick, spawns a visual "Marker" far off-screen.
Resolution: When the player ship gets within X distance of the Marker, destroy Marker and instantiate Enemy Ship entity.
9. AI
Navigation: AI keeps an invisible "Orbit Point" that circles the player at a preferred distance. AI ship just uses basic thrust/turn to drive toward that point.
Types:
Merchant: Drives directly away from player.
Pirate: Uses Orbit Point to circle and broadside.
Navy: Aggressive pursuit, orbits closer.
10. Boarding
Trigger: Close proximity + slow relative speed + hit Spacebar.
Resolution: Instant math check (Player Boarding Power vs Enemy Boarding Power + RNG).
Victory Options:
Salvage: Take Gold/Cargo, sink enemy.
Take Ship: Instant transfer. Swap player to new hull, auto-transfer surviving crew/supplies, abandon old ship.
11. Bounty (0-100)
Increases by: Attacking merchants, sinking navy.
Effects: Higher navy spawn rates, earlier merchant fleeing.
Decreases by: Paying gold at the Cove.
12. The Cove (Meta Hub)
UI: Strict DOM/HTML menus overlaying the canvas.
Tavern: Hire Crew, Pay off Bounty.
Shipwright: Repair Hull/Sails (Gold), Buy/Sell Ships, Upgrades.
Merchant: Buy Food/Rum/Powder, Sell Cargo.
Upgrades (3 Tiers): Hull (HP), Sails (Speed/Turn), Artillery (Reload/Spread), Magazine (Powder Cap), Quarters (Crew Cap).
13. Technical Architecture
Vanilla HTML5 Canvas 2D.
Fixed-timestep requestAnimationFrame loop (Accumulator pattern).
Save state is a simple JSON object of the player's stats/ship.
Object pooling for projectiles and particles.
